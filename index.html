⚠️ Interpretation guidance

For delays ≥ 12 h or uncertain storage temperatures, interpret N₀ as an interval estimate. Temperature is the dominant uncertainty driver (sensitivity analyses, published study). A ±2°C error in T has greater impact than uncertainty in k or λ.

About the equation

MM‑VCM inverse equation (closed‑form back‑calculation):

\[ N_0 = \frac{K \cdot N_t \cdot e^{k \cdot dt}}{N_t \cdot e^{k \cdot dt} + (K - N_t \cdot e^{k \cdot dt}) \cdot e^{r(T) \cdot dt}} \]

with \[ dt = \max(0,\, t - \lambda) \],   \[ r(T) = \frac{\ln 2}{G_{\text{base}}} \cdot \exp\!\left(-\frac{(T-37)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) \]

Assumptions & limitations
  • Assumes continuous logistic growth during effective period dt; intermittent temperature fluctuations are not captured.
  • Lag phase λ is fixed per profile; true lag varies with inoculum size and prior stress.
  • No output clamping — values <0 indicate an edge case (Nt > K after decay correction); interpret as N₀ ≈ 0.
  • Parameters validated for the eight listed organism–matrix pairs. Extrapolation requires local validation.
  • Not a substitute for clinical judgment or local laboratory validation.
Clinical notes & safeguards
  • When t ≤ λ, dt = 0 and N₀ = Nt (no correction applied).
  • Defaults reflect organism–matrix literature ranges validated in the published study.
  • Override any parameter with locally derived values for site-specific validation.
  • For clinical documentation, report N₀ alongside input parameters and delay time.

Quick guidance

1) Choose organism–matrix profile → Apply defaults.  2) Enter Nt, delay t, and temperature T.  3) Override λ, k, K, Gbase, σ for local data if available.  4) Click Compute N₀ to obtain N₀, dt, and r(T).
